Sports

Sports are crucial for holistic development, offering significant physical benefits like improved heart health, strength, and weight management, alongside vital mental health advantages such as stress reduction, better mood, and increased self-esteem. They also foster essential life skills like teamwork, discipline, communication, and leadership, contributing to better academic performance and social integration, while providing a sense of purpose and belonging.

Cricket is a globally popular bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players, involving batting, bowling, and fielding with the goal of scoring more runs than the opposition, featuring diverse formats like Test, One-Day (ODI), and T20, and embodying teamwork, discipline, and national pride, especially in countries like India, making it a significant cultural and sporting phenomenon.
Football (soccer) is a globally popular sport where two teams of 11 players try to score by getting a spherical ball into the opposing goal, using feet, head, or torso, but not hands (except the goalkeeper). Played in two 45-minute halves, it's governed by rules (Laws of the Game) focusing on skill, tactics, and athleticism, with fouls leading to free kicks or penalties, and players receiving yellow/red cards for misconduct. Beyond soccer, "football" also refers to codes like American Football, Australian Rules, and Rugby, but association football (soccer) is the most common.
